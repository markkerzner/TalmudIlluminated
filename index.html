Welcome to the Talmud Illuminated site

This site took seven-and-a-half years to compose. Every day I would learn a page of Talmud, in the order of Daf Yomi, and write a clear, concise description of the major topics discussed on that page. The Talmud covers many areas, from divorce to marriage (in this order), civil and criminal laws, and Temple service. It also contains many stories. By now, the project is complete, but I am still working on the improvements. This is my fourth time learning the complete 2711 pages of Talmud.

About myself. My name is Mark Kerzner, I live in Houston, TX, I am married with six children, and I have a cat and a dog fish. I am a full-time software developer and trainer in Big Data, and part-time many other things, such as learning the Talmud.

Why Talmud Illuminated?

I want to open knowledge to anybody to wants it. Let us say someone wants to know about Kiddushin (Jewish marriage). He can take the very good editions of Steinzalts or Artscroll, and study all of the eighty-two pages. This will take eight-two hours, or more, depending on the level of preparation. Or she can get the general idea by reading the pages on the Talmud Illuminated site, and then go ahead and deepen that knowledge.

Each page is described in about two hundred words. It is illustrated with a carefully selected painting from world art. It can serve as a knowledge journey, or art journey or both. You can subscribe to a daily email, and after seven years ofdaily reading

But why study the Talmud?

I personally two reasons. First, I wanted to have the knowledge. Second, learning is an excellent mental gymnastics. When I started a while back, I had this idea: "I am reasonably astute now, but what will be later, when I grow older? Let me engage in daily learning, and see what happens years later." I am still doing it.
